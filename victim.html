1. What is QRLJacking?

Quick Response Code Login Jacking is a social engineering attack that hijacks accounts that use the "Login with QR code" feature.

  • The Hook: It leverages the convenience of scanning a QR code to log into services (like WhatsApp, Discord, or Banking apps) on a desktop browser.
  • The Goal: The attacker hijacks your active session, gaining full control of your account without needing your username or password.

2. How the Attack Works (Anatomy of a Scam)

Attacker Sets Up: The attacker opens a legitimate service (e.g., WhatsApp Web) and gets a valid login QR code.
Attacker Clones: The attacker captures this QR code and immediately displays it on a fake, malicious website.
The Bait: The attacker sends you a phishing link (via email, SMS, or a "free offer" ad) telling you to scan the code.
The Scam: You scan the QR code from the malicious site with your mobile app.
The Hijack: Your app tells the website to "log in," but you are logging in the attacker.

Key takeaway: You are scanning a legitimate code that belongs to an attacker's session.

3. Real-Life Examples & Risks

  • Messaging Hijack: Attackers take over WhatsApp or Discord to spread malware or scam contacts.
  • Banking/Ecommerce Theft: Accessing banking apps (like Alipay) to authorize fraudulent transactions.
  • Data Exposure: Attackers can see your contacts, personal messages, and even your GPS location.

4. How to Defend Yourself

  • Treat QR Codes Like Links: Do not scan QR codes from unknown or untrusted sources.
  • Verify the Source: Only scan QR codes on official websites, especially when logging in.
  • Check the URL: Before scanning, make sure the URL in your browser is the real, official site (e.g., whatsapp.com).
  • Check Logged-In Sessions: Regularly check "Linked Devices" in your app settings.
  • Enable MFA: Use Multi-Factor Authentication whenever possible.

Why It Works: Evasion and Human Trust

  • Invisible to Filters: 70% of QR code attacks now use PDF attachments to bypass traditional security filters.
  • User Susceptibility: 73% of users admit to scanning QR codes without verifying the destination first. e.g If you in Sikkim but location is showing Delhi, Kolkota etc. then it is a suspicious activity.
