What is QRLJacking?

Quick Response Login Jacking is a session hijacking technique. Instead of stealing your password, the attacker "relays" a legitimate login QR code to you. When you scan it, you aren't logging yourself in—you are authorizing the attacker's device to access your account.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Scanning QR codes from unsolicited emails or SMS messages.
  • Scanning QR stickers in public places (parking, menus) without checking if they were pasted over.
  • Ignoring the "Confirm Login?" prompt on your phone which shows the device location.
  • Assuming that QR codes are inherently more "secure" than passwords.

How to Stay Safe

  • Check the URL: Before authorizing, check the link displayed by your scanner.
  • Verify Location: If the login prompt says "Login from Mumbai" and you are in Delhi, reject it immediately.
  • Use Official Apps: Only use official apps for scanning sensitive logins.
  • Enable MFA: Use hardware keys or Authenticator apps that require an extra tap after the scan.
